Capsule 31 – Les crampes (moutarde et cornichons)
PMID: 32459412
On entend souvent parler… des crampes en sport d’endurance, mais il existe un remèdes efficace qui permet d’annuler littéralement les crampes lorsqu’elles surviennent. Le jus de cornichon et la moutarde.
Allons voir la littérature… Des essais cliniques montrent que boire seulement quelques gorgées de jus de cornichon ou de moutarde, au début d’une crampe, peut réduire la durée de celle-ci de près de 40 % en moyenne, comparé à l’eau. Cet effet est observé en moins de 2 minutes, voir même quelques secondes, ce qui est beaucoup trop rapide pour être lié à un apport d’électrolytes. Le mécanisme est plutôt liée au système nerveux : l’acide acétique stimule des récepteurs dans la bouche et la gorge, envoyant un signal qui inhibe les motoneurones alpha responsables de la contraction involontaire. Pour la moutarde, les évidences sont moins fréquentes, mais elle contient les mêmes composés clés qui permet d’avoir cet effet.
En somme… ces stratégies peuvent fonctionner ponctuellement, sans risque particulier à petite dose. Par contre, elles ne préviennent pas la fatigue musculaire ni la déshydratation qui sont les causes de la crampe elle -même! La crampe peut donc revenir si le pace ou l’hydratation n’est pas ajusté. C’est un outil à avoir dans ta prochaine course ou épreuve d’endurance!

Chez plusieurs influenceurs fitness, même quand l’objectif est noble en voulant motiver son public, entretenir un discours qui présente la perte de poids comme un question de volonté nuit à la santé des individus qui le recoit. Je serai le premier à l’admettre. On a baigné et grandie dans un monde ou on s’est fait répéter et imprégner ce message. Mais ça ne veut pas dire quon doit laisser ces penser affecter nos interactions et nos relations. On doit activement les combattre avec des réflexions comme celle-ci.
En somme… la science est claire : l’obésité n’est pas un choix, mais un état multifactoriel. Les discours grossophobes ont littéralement aucune base scientifique et nuisent directement à la santé publique, contrairement à la pensé populaire.

Capsule 33 – Les cafés protéinés et le marketing

On entend récemment parler… des cafés protéinés chez Tim ou des aliments « enrichie en protéines » qu’on retrouve maintenant partout. Est-ce vraiment utile? 
Allons voir la littérature… Pour les compagnies, les allégations « riche en protéines » augmentent nettement l’intention d’achat, même si le produit est riche en sucre, en gras ou en sodium. Par exemple, des analyses ont montré que plus de 50 % des aliments affichant « protéiné » étaient classés comme « moins sains » selon les critères nutritionnels.  Les consommateurs associent automatiquement « protéine » à santé, performance et satiété. Chez les athlètes, cette perception est encore plus marquée, alors que chez les personnes âgées, on observe une méfiance envers les produits « artificiellement enrichis ». On a avantage à consommer des protéines régulièrement dans notre journée mais le café protéiné est le summun du coup marketing qui profite d’une tendance. Protéiné n’égale pas santé et on a aucunement besoin de TOUS nos aliments contiennent des protéines. On a pas besoin que nos croustilles soit protéiné, que nos boissons soit protéiné, ou que nimporte quelle aliment soit enrichie en protéine. 
En somme… les protéine sont essentielles comme on a pu en discuter dans les capsules récente, mais 10g de protéine ne transforme pas magiquement un produit comme étant meilleur pour la santé. Bien sur tu peux facilement apprécier ces aliments, mais sachant que c’est une opération marketing associé à une mode temporaire. Il faut replacer la protéine dans son contexte : les aliments protéinés (volaille, poisson, les produits de soya, lait, yogourt, œufs, légumineuses) doivent faire partie intégrante de notre alimentation et ils offrent beaucoup plus à l’alimentation que ces produits sans avoir besoin d’un branding sur une étiquette.

Capsule 34 – Protéines animales vs végétales
PMID: 39813010
On entend récemment parler… de la supériorité supposée des protéines animales sur les végétales. Mais est-ce encore vrai à la lumière des recherches récentes ?
Allons voir la littérature… Les protéines animales ont en moyenne une digestibilité plus élevée et une teneur supérieure en acides aminés essentiels, en particulier la leucine, qui est clé pour activer la synthèse musculaire. Les études montrent qu’une dose de 20 g de protéines animales stimule plus efficacement cette réponse que la même dose de soya ou de blé, surtout chez les personnes âgées. Et oui, les protéines végétales prisent seule sont incomplètes. Par contre, il est assez simple de faire de la complémentarité des source de protéine. Par exemple un grain et un produit de soya procure au corps toutes les acides aminées essentiel dont il a besoin. Lorsque les protéines végétales sont combinée,, leur efficacité devient alors identique dans les études sur le sujet. Des méta-analyses confirment que les gains en masse et en force musculaire sont similaires quand l’apport protéique total est adéquat.
En somme… les protéines animals on un avantage ou niveau de la simplicité, mais les protéines végétal peuvent parfaitement remplir son rôle si on a l’éducations en nutrition nous permettant de bâtir des repas complet. Sachant que les protéines végétales ont plusieurs bénéfices autres, notamment au niveau de l’environnement et du porte feuille, on peut substituer des sources de protéine animal pour des source de protéine végétale sans aucun stress au quotidien.

Capsule 35 – Microbiote et santé
PMID: 35105664
On entend récemment parler… du microbiote comme du « nouvel organe méconnu », au cœur de notre immunité et même de notre humeur. La recherche est très active dans ce domaine, mais aussi la vente de supplément diverse pour en favoriser le développeemnt.
Allons voir la littérature… Le microbiote intestinal, composé de milliards de bactéries, participe à la production de vitamines, à la fermentation des fibres, et à la régulation de l’immunité. Un microbiote diversifié est un signe de résilience. À l’inverse, une dysbiose est associée à l’obésité, au diabète, aux maladies inflammatoires chroniques de l’intestin, et même à des troubles neurologiques. Les mécanismes incluent une inflammation chronique, des perturbations dans la production d’acides gras à chaîne courte et une perméabilité accrue de la barrière intestinale. Donc oui, on veut favoriser une micripbiote intestinale sain. Malgré qu’il existent plusieurs suppléement à cet effet, les interventions les plus solides de la littérature restent l’alimentation riche en fibres et en végétaux, qui favorisent la diversité microbienne. Ces suppléemnt ne sont donc pas mauvais en soit, mais peu voir pas utile dans un contexte d’alimentation variée. Certain population comme les athlète de haut volume, à risque d’une snaté immunitaire diminué, peuvent bénéficier de tel suppléemnt pour réduire l’incidence de certain virus.
En somme… prendre soin de son microbiote c’est à la mode mais vrai. ceoendant ce n’est pas acheter un probiotique miracle, mais plutôt nourrir quotidiennement sa flore avec une alimentation variée et peu transformée. C’est une stratégie accessible, efficace et soutenue par la science.

Mustard and Pickle Juice for Muscle Cramp Relief: What Does the Research Say?
Mustard and pickle juice are popular home remedies for muscle cramps, especially among athletes and patients with chronic conditions. Recent research has explored their effectiveness, possible mechanisms, and safety, providing a clearer picture of their potential benefits and limitations.
Mechanisms of Action
Pickle juice and mustard both contain acetic acid and sodium, which are thought to help relieve cramps. The relief is likely not due to rapid electrolyte replacement, but rather a neurally mediated reflex triggered by oropharyngeal stimulation, which may inhibit the firing of alpha motor neurons responsible for cramping 16.
Effectiveness in Reducing Cramp Severity
Pickle juice: Randomized controlled trials in patients with cirrhosis and experimental studies in athletes show that sipping pickle juice at cramp onset can significantly reduce cramp duration and severity compared to water, with effects seen within minutes 569.
Mustard: Contains similar acetic acid and sodium content as pickle juice, and is considered a practical and palatable option for cramp relief, though direct evidence for its effectiveness is less robust 134.
Both remedies are widely used by athletes and patients, with patient reports supporting their perceived benefit 7.
Safety and Limitations
Neither pickle juice nor mustard exacerbates electrolyte imbalances or causes adverse events when used in small amounts. However, they do not fully replenish fluid or electrolyte losses after intense exercise or dehydration 34.Most studies focus on short-term relief, and more research is needed on long-term safety and optimal dosing 9.

GLP-1 Agonists and Long-Term Weight Loss: Sustained Efficacy, Challenges, and Considerations
Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs) have transformed obesity management, offering significant weight loss for people with and without diabetes. Recent research focuses on their long-term effectiveness, safety, and the challenges of maintaining weight loss after discontinuation.
Long-Term Weight Loss Efficacy
GLP-1 RAs such as semaglutide, liraglutide, and tirzepatide consistently produce substantial weight loss over 1–2 years, with average reductions ranging from 5% to over 20% of baseline body weight depending on the agent and dose 1143591920. Longer treatment cycles and higher baseline BMI are associated with greater weight loss, and dual/triple agonists (e.g., tirzepatide, retatrutide) outperform single GLP-1 agents 114319. However, real-world studies show that only a minority of patients maintain ≥5% weight loss at 1–2 years, partly due to adherence and discontinuation issues 74.
Weight Maintenance and Discontinuation
Sustaining weight loss typically requires ongoing GLP-1 RA therapy; discontinuation often leads to significant weight regain 356. Lifelong or chronic treatment may be necessary for long-term weight control, similar to other chronic disease therapies 53. Weight regain is a major challenge, highlighting the need for strategies to support maintenance after stopping medication 356.
Safety and Adverse Effects
GLP-1 RAs are generally well tolerated, with most adverse events being mild-to-moderate gastrointestinal symptoms (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea) 13518. Serious adverse events and discontinuations due to side effects are rare, but long-term safety data are still accumulating 13516.

Introduction Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs) are a type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) treatment with demonstrated weight loss benefits in clinical trials. However, the extent to which real-world patients with T2DM achieve clinically meaningful weight loss (≥5%) has not been well characterized. Analysis of real-world data suggests adherence to injectable GLP-1 RAs is suboptimal and discontinuation following the first year of therapy is poorly characterized. Research design and methods A retrospective cohort study among patients with T2DM initiating injectable GLP-1 RA therapy was conducted using the Clinical Practice Research Datalink that includes primary care medical records for 13 million patients in the UK. This study assessed weight change, adherence (proportion of days covered (PDC) ≥80%), and discontinuation (≥90-day gap between prescriptions) at 12 and 24 months during the study period spanning January 2009–December 2017. Results Among 589 patients initiating a GLP-1 RA, 56.4% were female and the median age was 54 years (IQR (46, 61)). The median body mass index was 41.2 kg/m2 (IQR (35.8, 46.4)). Among patients with weight measures available (n=341 at 12 months; n=232 at 24 months), 33.4% and 43.5% achieved weight loss ≥5% of baseline weight at 12 and 24 months, respectively. At 12 and 24 months, 64.5% and 59.2% were adherent, and 45.2% and 64.7% discontinued, respectively. Conclusions A minority of patients initiating GLP-1 RAs achieved ≥5% weight loss, suggesting the real-world benefit of these agents on weight loss may be lower than that observed in clinical trials. Patients on GLP-1 RAs may benefit from additional support to improve long-term adherence.

Worldwide, nearly 40% of adults are overweight and 13% are obese. Health consequences of excess weight include cardiovascular diseases, type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia, and increased mortality. Treating obesity is challenging and calorie restriction often leads to rebound weight gain. Treatments such as bariatric surgery create hesitancy among patients due to their invasiveness. GLP-1 medications have revolutionized weight loss and can reduce body weight in obese patients by between 15% and 25% on average after about 1 year. Their mode of action is to mimic the endogenous GLP-1, an intestinal hormone that regulates glucose metabolism and satiety. However, GLP-1 drugs carry known risks and, since their use for weight loss is recent, may carry unforeseen risks as well. They carry a boxed warning for people with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2. Gastrointestinal adverse events (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea) are fairly common while pancreatitis and intestinal obstruction are rarer. There may be a loss of lean body mass as well as premature facial aging. A significant disadvantage of using these medications is the high rate of weight regain when they are discontinued. Achieving success with pharmacologic treatment and then weaning to avoid future negative effects would be ideal.

Reasons for low levels of initiation and long-term use of AOMs may include reluctance of public health and medical organizations to recognize obesity as a disease, lack of reimbursement, provider inexperience, and misperceptions about the efficacy and safety of available treatments. This article aims to inform primary care providers about the mechanism of action of one class of AOMs, glucagon-like peptide 1 receptor agonists (GLP-1RAs), in weight loss and longer-term maintenance of weight loss, and the efficacy and safety of this treatment class. GLP-1RA therapy was initially developed to treat type 2 diabetes. Owing to their effectiveness in reducing body weight, once-daily subcutaneous administration of liraglutide 3.0 mg has been approved, and once-weekly subcutaneous administration of semaglutide 2.4 mg is being investigated in phase III trials, for obesity management. Considerations regarding adverse effects and contraindications for different drug classes are provided to help guide treatment decision-making when considering pharmacotherapy for weight management in patients with obesity. Obesity is a growing public health issue that increases the risk of developing heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and osteoarthritis. Weight loss can reduce the risk of developing these health problems but, despite this, levels of obesity remain high. Achieving and maintaining weight loss is challenging for many individuals. There is therefore a need for some patients to take medications to help them lose weight and prevent weight regain. Glucagon-like peptide 1 receptor agonists (GLP-1RAs) are a type of medication originally developed to treat type 2 diabetes, but are now being used for the treatment of obesity because they are effective at helping people to lose weight. One GLP-1RA, liraglutide, has been approved to treat obesity, and another, semaglutide, is in clinical trials. GLP-1RAs work by reducing the appetite and feelings of hunger, slowing the release of food from the stomach, and increasing feelings of fullness after eating. Most people can tolerate GLP-1RAs well. The most common side effects (nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea) are usually mild and occur in the first few weeks of treatment, reducing over time. Because of the difficulties many people face in maintaining weight loss, lifelong treatment may be needed. In clinical trials, GLP-1RAs were well tolerated and effective at helping people prevent weight regain, and may be a good option for long-term weight control and lowering patients' chances of serious health problems.

Marketing in High-Protein Foods: Trends, Strategies, and Consumer Perceptions
High-protein foods have become a major focus in the food industry, driven by consumer beliefs in their health and fitness benefits. Marketing strategies for these products often leverage health claims, packaging design, and product innovation to influence consumer choices, even as the actual health benefits and nutritional quality of many high-protein foods remain debated.
Marketing Strategies and Claims
Marketing of high-protein foods frequently emphasizes protein content through front-of-pack labeling, positive nutrient claims, and scientific-sounding information, often regardless of the actual healthiness of the product 1210.Protein-related claims are the most common positive nutrient claim on alternative protein products, and packaging often uses design elements to suggest health, science, and lifestyle benefits 110. However, these claims can be misleading, as many products with protein claims are classified as "less healthy" due to high fat, sodium, or sweetener content 5.
Consumer Perceptions and Behavior
Consumers generally perceive high-protein foods as healthier and more beneficial for physical performance and body composition, despite limited scientific evidence supporting these beliefs 156. The presence of "high-protein" claims increases willingness to purchase and enhances perceptions of quality and eco-friendliness, especially for plant-based options 6. However, many consumers struggle to distinguish between naturally high-protein foods and those with added protein, and older consumers are more skeptical of artificially increased protein content 7.
Market Trends and Industry Responses
The high-protein food sector is rapidly expanding, with both traditional and alternative protein sources (plant-based, microalgae, insects) gaining market share 8917. Companies are innovating with new product formats and targeting specific consumer segments, such as athletes, who show a higher willingness to pay for functional high-protein foods 410. Industry convergence around "protein" as a marketing focus is also reshaping food system dynamics 15.



Capsules 34 – Les protéines animales sont-elles supérieur au protéine végétales ?
Animal vs. Plant-Based Protein: Impacts on Health and Muscle Synthesis
Interest in plant-based proteins is rising due to health and environmental benefits, but questions remain about their effectiveness compared to animal proteins for muscle synthesis and overall health. Research has focused on protein quality, muscle mass, and strength outcomes, especially in the context of exercise and aging.
Muscle Protein Synthesis and Anabolic Potential
Animal proteins generally have higher digestibility and essential amino acid (EAA) content, especially leucine, which is crucial for muscle protein synthesis. Most studies show animal proteins stimulate muscle protein synthesis more effectively than single-source plant proteins like soy or wheat, particularly in older adults or when protein intake is low 1357817. However, plant-based blends enriched with limiting amino acids (e.g., lysine, methionine, leucine) or higher total protein doses can match the anabolic response of animal proteins in young adults 191112161920.
Effects on Muscle Mass and Strength
Meta-analyses and systematic reviews indicate that both animal and plant proteins support increases in muscle mass and strength, but animal protein may offer a slight advantage in percent lean mass, especially in younger adults 23. For muscle strength and physical performance, differences between protein sources are minimal when total protein intake is adequate 231518.
Health and Dietary Considerations
Plant-based proteins are associated with lower saturated fat and higher fiber intake, potentially reducing risks of obesity, heart disease, and some cancers 413. The total amount of protein consumed, rather than the animal-to-plant ratio, is a stronger predictor of muscle mass in older adults 14. Combining plant proteins or fortifying them with EAAs can improve their quality 17817.

Capsules 36 – Le microbiote et la santé
Microbiota and Human Health: An Integrated Overview
The human microbiota—trillions of microorganisms inhabiting the gut, skin, mouth, and other body sites—plays a fundamental role in maintaining health. These microbial communities influence metabolism, immunity, and even neurological function, while disruptions (dysbiosis) are linked to a wide range of diseases. Understanding the mechanisms and clinical implications of microbiota-host interactions is a rapidly advancing field.
Roles of Microbiota in Health
Metabolic and Immune Regulation: Microbiota aid in nutrient extraction, vitamin synthesis, and energy metabolism, and are essential for the development and regulation of the immune system. A diverse and balanced microbiota supports resilience against pathogens and environmental stressors 145101119.
Gut-Brain and Gut-Organ Axes: The gut microbiota communicates with the brain and other organs via metabolic, hormonal, and immune pathways, influencing mood, cognition, and systemic health 171119.
Microbiota Dysbiosis and Disease
Associated Conditions: Dysbiosis is linked to metabolic disorders (obesity, diabetes), cardiovascular diseases, neurodegenerative diseases, inflammatory bowel disease, and even some cancers 124568912+1 MORE.
Mechanisms: Disease associations involve chronic inflammation, immune dysregulation, altered metabolite production (e.g., short-chain fatty acids), and impaired barrier function 456101213.
Defining and Modulating a Healthy Microbiota
Healthy Microbiome Features: High diversity, stability, and the presence of beneficial microbial guilds are hallmarks of health, though individual variability is significant 1101517.
Interventions: Diet, prebiotics, probiotics, and fecal microbiota transplantation are being explored to restore or enhance microbiota composition and function 151019.


Capsules 37 – Est-ce que suivre le nouveau Guide est un complot gouvernementale ?
The 2019 Canadian Food Guide and Health: Evidence and Implications
The 2019 Canada’s Food Guide (CFG) emphasizes healthy food choices and eating habits, aiming to reduce chronic disease risk and improve overall health in Canadians. Recent research has begun to assess its health impacts, particularly regarding cardiometabolic outcomes and nutrient adequacy.
Health Outcomes and Chronic Disease Risk
Greater adherence to the 2019 CFG recommendations is associated with significant reductions in cardiovascular disease (CVD) risk. A large prospective analysis found that increasing adherence to CFG recommendations, as measured by the Healthy Eating Food Index (HEFI)-2019, could reduce the 11-year risk of CVD by 24% in middle-aged and older adults 4. Additional national survey data show that Canadians with diets more closely aligned to the 2019 CFG have lower body mass index, improved cholesterol profiles, and reduced markers of inflammation, all of which are linked to lower cardiometabolic risk 6. Ongoing Canadian cohort studies are expected to further clarify the relationship between CFG adherence and CVD risk, as well as potential healthcare cost savings 3.
Nutrient Adequacy and Special Populations
For older adults, higher adherence to CFG recommendations is linked to reduced inadequacy of key nutrients such as magnesium, vitamin B6, and protein. However, there is evidence that following the guide may not ensure sufficient intake of folate, vitamin D, and calcium, nutrients of particular concern for older populations 518. This suggests that while the guide supports overall nutrient adequacy, targeted strategies may be needed for certain groups.
Broader Health and Policy Implications
The 2019 CFG aligns well with international healthy and sustainable dietary patterns, such as the EAT-Lancet reference diet, supporting both health and environmental goals 17. The guide’s focus on reducing ultra-processed foods and promoting whole, minimally processed foods is consistent with global recommendations for chronic disease prevention 20. However, barriers to adoption remain, including affordability concerns and cultural relevance, especially among diverse and newcomer populations 819.
